# Player Guide

This guide is for players joining a session where the GM is using DungeonPlanner maps.

## What players should expect

- Clear floor layouts with walls and openings
- Visual props that help communicate scene context
- Camera views that make positioning and line-of-sight easier to read

## Reading the map quickly

- **Top-down view** is best for tactical positioning.
- **Isometric view** is best for room shape and elevation context.
- **Doors/openings** indicate traversable boundaries.
- **Lighting props** often mark points of interest.

## During play

- Ask your GM to switch to **top-down** for movement-heavy turns.
- Ask for **isometric/perspective** when spatial feel matters (height, cover, room shape).
- Use landmarks (torch, pillar, rubble, doorway) to communicate intent clearly.

## Common terms

- **Cell**: one grid tile.
- **Layer**: a visibility group for map elements.
- **Floor**: level index (ground, upper floors, cellar).
